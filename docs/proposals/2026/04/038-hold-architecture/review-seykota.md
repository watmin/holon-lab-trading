# Review: Seykota

Verdict: CONDITIONAL

---

You have a fee problem. You correctly identified it. The system captures 0.17% and pays 0.70%. That arithmetic kills you regardless of win rate. Holding is the right instinct — you cannot scalp through a 0.70% round-trip. So the direction is sound. But the proposal has confusion in it that will hurt you.

**Accumulation zones are not trend following. They are prediction.** You say "high conviction at an extreme = the bottom is forming." That is calling the bottom. I have never called a bottom in my life. The trend follower buys breakouts — price proving itself by moving. Your market observer should signal "something has started moving" not "something is forming." Forming is hope. Moving is data. If your readiness signal fires at the bottom before the move, you are predicting. If it fires when the move confirms, you are reacting. The proposal describes prediction dressed as readiness.

**50 entries at the bottom is hesitation, not diversification.** Diversification means uncorrelated bets across instruments or timeframes. Fifty papers on the same asset at the same bottom is one bet with fifty execution slices. If the bottom holds, you wanted one big entry. If it fails, fifty small losses are still one loss. Call it what it is: you are unsure, so you spread the commitment. That is fine — sizing into a position is sound risk management — but do not confuse it with portfolio theory.

**The trailing stop distance is the whole game.** The proposal says nothing about what determines it. ATR is the minimum answer. A fixed percentage is lazy but honest. A volatility-regime-adjusted trail is better — tight in quiet markets, wide in volatile ones. Your exit observer should learn this, and I believe it can. But "expected future residue" is unknowable. Use what price has already shown you: recent range, recent volatility. The trail should breathe with the market, not predict the future.

**Holding solves the fee problem only if you hold winners long enough.** The math works at 5% captures. But if your exit observer exits at 1.5% because a retracement spooked it, you pay 0.70% on 1.5% — still marginal. The default-is-hold discipline must be enforced structurally, not aspirationally. The trailing stop is the right mechanism. But the stop must be wide enough that noise does not trigger it, and tight enough that a real reversal does not eat the profit. That tension is the craft.

**"The default is HOLD" — I agree completely.** This is the single most important sentence in the proposal. Most systems overtrade. The profitable trade is the one you are already in. Every exit is a fee. Every re-entry is a fee. Hold until the market proves you wrong. The trailing stop is the proof mechanism. Good.

## Answers to the five questions

1. **Discrete vs continuous exit:** Continuous. Predict the distance. The holding period emerges from the distance — wider trail, longer hold. Discrete "hold or exit" loses information.

2. **Papers at every candle or threshold:** Threshold. Fewer entries, larger size, fewer fees. But the threshold must be learned, not fixed.

3. **Treasury managing portfolio vs independent brokers:** Treasury. Capital allocation across brokers is where edge compounds. Independent brokers cannot manage total exposure.

4. **Expected future residue — learnable?** No. Use a proxy. ATR-scaled trail distance. The exit observer learns the trail multiplier, not the future.

5. **Higher lows — separate signal or sustained conviction?** Same observer, sustained conviction. A new signal implies a new prediction. Sustained conviction is "the move is still moving." That is reaction, not prediction. Keep it as one signal.

The system has the right bones. Fix the entry from prediction to reaction. Make the trail breathe. Hold the winners. Cut the losers. The fees solve themselves.
