# Review: Wyckoff

**Verdict: Approve with reservations. The accumulator school captures the skeleton of the method but lacks its circulatory system — volume.**

## The five questions

1. **Replace or coexist?** Replace. The current lenses are four copies of the same mind. A vocabulary difference that produces identical behavior is not diversity. Kill them. The schools are the real thing.

2. **Trade state: paper or exit observer?** The paper. Trade state is fact — price entered, excursion achieved, higher lows counted. Facts belong where they are observed, not where they are interpreted. The exit observer READS the paper's state and THINKS about it through its atoms. The paper measures. The school judges.

3. **Four atoms enough?** Four atoms is correct for the exit. The market observer carries 20-100 atoms because the market is complex. The exit question is simple: hold or leave. Four measurements of the trade's health is sufficient. More atoms would make the exit observer a second market observer. That is not its job.

4. **Fee in vocabulary or gate?** The gate. The patient school encoding the fee directly is a design error. The fee is a constant — it does not vary with market conditions. Constants belong in the treasury's acceptance logic, not in the exit's learned representation. The patient school should encode hold-value-proxy and time-decay. The treasury should enforce the fee gate.

5. **Shared atoms via extraction?** No. The market observer's atoms (RSI, ATR, regime) already flow into the exit through the COMPOSED thought — the broker binds market thought to exit thought. Extraction gives the exit observer its own copy of what the market observer already said. Redundancy. The exit schools should think ONLY about the trade, not about the market. The market observer thinks about the market. That is why there are two roles.

## The accumulator school

The skeleton is there. Higher-low-count is the test after the spring. Ratchet-progress is the markup phase — the floor rising as the trend confirms. Retracement-ratio distinguishes noise from distribution. These are sound measurements.

What is missing is the PHASE. Accumulation is not a single state. It is a sequence: preliminary support, selling climax, automatic rally, secondary test, spring, test of the spring, sign of strength, last point of support, then markup. The accumulator school collapses this entire sequence into "higher-low-count." That loses the narrative.

But — this system does not need the narrative. The market observer detects the formation. The exit observer manages the position AFTER entry. By the time the accumulator school is active, the spring has already fired. The entry happened. The school's job is to hold through the markup and exit when distribution begins. For that purpose, ratchet-progress and retracement-acceleration are the right measurements. A lower low breaking the ratchet floor IS the sign of distribution.

## Volume

Its absence is not fatal, but it is a wound. Volume confirms every phase transition. A spring on low volume is not a spring — it is a breakdown. A markup on declining volume is distribution in disguise. The volume lens exists in the market observers. If the market observer uses volume to confirm entry, and the exit observer uses price structure to manage the hold, the division is defensible. Volume confirms intent. Price confirms state.

The danger: the exit observer cannot distinguish a low-volume retracement (healthy) from a high-volume retracement (distribution) without volume atoms. Consider adding one: `volume-ratio (/ retracement-volume markup-volume)`.

## The spring

The system buys at "conviction extremes." This is not the spring. This is the selling climax — the moment of maximum fear. The spring is the RE-TEST of the low on lighter volume. The conviction extreme is the climax; the sustained conviction is the test. The system conflates them, but the hold architecture partly compensates — deploying many papers at the bottom means some catch the climax, some catch the test, some catch the sign of strength. The portfolio IS the phase detection.

## Can a machine learn accumulation without the composite operator?

Yes. The composite operator is a narrative convenience — "smart money" acting with intent. The machine does not need to model intent. It needs to model the FOOTPRINT of intent: volume patterns, price structure, duration. The atoms encode the footprint. The reckoner learns which footprints precede Grace. The composite operator is implicit in the data. The machine finds the pattern without naming the cause.
