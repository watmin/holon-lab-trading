# Review: Van Tharp

Verdict: **Conditional approval.** This is the proposal 038 needed to become real. But the math has constraints you must respect.

## The five questions

**1. Replace or coexist?** Replace. The current lenses produce identical management behavior. Keeping them alongside schools creates noise without signal. Kill the old lenses. The schools ARE the lenses now.

**2. Trade state on the paper or the observer?** The paper. The paper is the trade record. Excursion, higher-low-count, candles-since-entry -- these are facts about the trade, not opinions about the market. The observer reads trade state; it does not own it.

**3. Four atoms enough?** Barely. See below.

**4. Fee in vocabulary or gate?** Vocabulary. The patient school's entire philosophy is fee-awareness. Removing the fee from its atoms lobotomizes it. The treasury gate is a hard constraint. The observer's fee atom is a soft thought. Both should exist. The observer thinks about fees. The treasury enforces them.

**5. Share market atoms or own them?** Share through extraction. The exit observer should not re-encode RSI. It should read the market observer's thought. The broker already pairs them -- let the exit compose with the market thought, not duplicate it. This is how you get dimensionality without redundant atoms.

## Diversification of 24 strategies

Yes, but only if the schools genuinely produce different holding periods. Trend-follower holds days. Swing holds hours. Patient holds until fee-breakeven. Accumulator holds through retracements. If these four philosophies produce four distinct holding period distributions, you have real diversification of returns across time horizons. If they converge to the same behavior under selection pressure, you have 24 copies of one strategy paying 24 entry fees. Monitor the holding period distributions. The moment two schools converge, one is redundant.

## Minimum sample size per broker

At least 30 resolved trades before the curve means anything. At 100 you can distinguish a 55% win rate from 50% with reasonable confidence. With 24 brokers needing 100 trades each, that is 2,400 resolved trades before selection pressure is meaningful. At 5-minute candles with longer holding periods, budget accordingly. The danger: premature selection kills a slow school before it has enough samples.

## Fee multiplication

24 brokers entering simultaneously is catastrophic. If all six market observers fire on the same candle, you pay 24 entry fees on one signal. The treasury must throttle. Fund proven brokers first. Fund unproven brokers with minimal size. The diversification benefit is real only if the entries are temporally distributed. If they cluster, the fees destroy you.

## Four atoms per school

This is tight. A reckoner with four linear inputs has a 4-dimensional thought space. It can learn, but its discrimination is coarse. The answer from question 5 saves you: compose the exit thought with the market thought. The exit observer thinks 4 atoms of management plus whatever dimensionality it extracts from the market observer's thought. That gives you 4 management dimensions riding on top of 20-100 market dimensions. The management atoms select HOW to hold. The market atoms provide the context OF the hold. Together: sufficient.

Do not add more atoms to the schools. Four is a feature, not a limitation. Each school should be a simple philosophy. Complexity belongs in the population, not the individual. You said this in the proposal. Hold to it.
