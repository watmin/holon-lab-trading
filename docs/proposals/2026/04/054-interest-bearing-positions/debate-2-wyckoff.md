# Debate Round 2: Wyckoff

**Verdict: APPROVE — with one structural warning.**

## What this proposal gets right

The composite man operates through accumulation and distribution.
This proposal finally understands that. The phase labeler detecting
three consecutive higher lows or lower highs — that is accumulation
and distribution made legible to a machine. The 53-candle average
window, the 44% active time — these are the campaigns I wrote about
a century ago. The market telegraphs its intentions through structure.
This proposal reads the structure.

The interest-as-stop-loss is the most honest mechanism I have seen in
this project. Distance-based exits were always the wrong frame. A
price level is arbitrary. The cost of holding is not. When I studied
the tape, the operators who survived were not the ones with clever
stop prices — they were the ones who understood the carrying cost of
being wrong. Interest selects for conviction that is correct. It
kills conviction that is merely stubborn. This is the law of effort
versus result, expressed as economics.

## The headless treasury is correct

The treasury as blind arbiter — seeing outcomes, not strategy — is
the only architecture that scales to multiplayer. I operated in
markets with thousands of participants. No central authority knew
anyone's reasoning. The tape recorded actions. The account ledger
recorded results. The composite man does not care WHY you bought.
He cares WHETHER you were right.

The "earning favor" mechanism through paper survival is the modern
equivalent of a seat on the exchange floor. You earned it by
demonstrating competence, not by explaining your method. The
proposer who reveals nothing and produces Grace is funded. This is
how real markets have always worked.

## The structural warning

The proposal says papers resolve at trigger points or die when
interest exceeds position value. But it does not specify the
evaluation frequency for the interest-exceeds-value check. If
evaluation only happens at peaks and valleys, a position can be
deeply underwater between triggers — the interest clock ticking
while no evaluation occurs during transitions. The treasury must
evaluate interest erosion on every candle, not only at trigger
points. Grace requires a trigger. Violence does not. Violence is
arithmetic, and arithmetic does not wait for structure.

## On the questions

**Q1 (lending rate):** ATR-proportional. The rate must breathe with
volatility. A fixed rate is too permissive in quiet markets and too
punishing in volatile ones. The market sets the cost. Always has.

**Q3 (discrete vs continuous):** Discrete. "Exit or hold" is a
decision. "How much longer" is a prediction about the future, and
the future is not knowable. The position observer should decide at
each trigger, not forecast duration.

**Q9 (rebalancing risk):** The phase labeler's symmetry handles
this naturally. Buy windows and sell windows are nearly equal in
count (2,891 vs 2,843). The treasury will oscillate around balance.
If it drifts, the rate adjusts. Do not add directional limits —
they would be the treasury having an opinion about the market, and
the headless treasury must not think.

**Q10 (paper erosion as sole gate):** Sufficient. Adding secondary
checks is adding the treasury's judgment about what "good" looks
like beyond survival. Survival IS the test. The market is the
examiner. The interest is the passing grade. Do not second-guess
the examiner.

## The accumulation model holds

Deploy capital, recover principal, keep residue. Both directions.
The interest ensures only runners accumulate. The rest return
principal through Violence — the treasury is made whole, the
broker is made humble. This is accumulation through natural
selection. The composite man approves.

The proposal is ready to build.
