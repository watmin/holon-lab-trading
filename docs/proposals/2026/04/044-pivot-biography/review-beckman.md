# Review: Beckman
Verdict: Accept with architectural clarification

## Question 8: Sequential as AST form

The free algebra generated by `{Atom, Linear, Log, Circular, Bind, Bundle}`
has a specific structure. Let me be precise about what each generator does
categorically:

- `Atom`, `Linear`, `Log`, `Circular` are **nullary/unary constructors** --
  they inject values from external domains (strings, reals, angles) into
  the vector space. These are the "ground terms."
- `Bind` is a **binary product** -- it combines two vectors via a
  structure-preserving operation (XOR-like, reversible). This is your
  tensor product in the algebra.
- `Bundle` is an **n-ary coproduct** -- it superposes vectors via
  majority vote. This is your direct sum (up to the threshold
  nonlinearity).

Now: `Sequential(children)` = `Bundle(permute(child_0, 0), permute(child_1, 1), ...)`.

The question is whether `permute` is already expressible in the existing
generators. It is NOT. Permutation is a cyclic shift automorphism on the
vector space -- it is not `Bind`, it is not `Bundle`, it is not any
composition of the existing generators. The existing algebra has no way
to say "rotate the coordinate frame by k positions." You cannot build
`permute(v, 3)` from `bind`, `bundle`, and the scalar encoders.

However -- and this is the critical distinction -- `permute` is not
introduced AS a generator. It is introduced as a FIXED operation inside
the evaluation of `Sequential`. The user never writes `Permute(child, k)`.
The permutation index is positional, determined by the child's location in
the vector. This is not a free variable -- it is a structural property of
the form.

Categorically, what you have is this: `Sequential` is a **functor from
the category of finite ordered lists to the vector space**, where the
functor action on morphisms (reorderings) is non-trivial. `Bundle` is
the corresponding functor from **finite multisets** (unordered). They
share the same codomain but have different source categories. This is
genuinely new structure.

**My position:** `Sequential` deserves generator status. It is not
derivable from the existing generators. The permutation automorphism
it introduces is a new algebraic capability. The fact that the proposal
correctly identifies it as "sugar for permute + bundle" is technically
accurate at the level of the evaluation, but at the level of the FREE
algebra (the AST), it introduces ordered composition as a first-class
concept that Bundle cannot express.

The alternative -- `Bind(position_atom, thought)` pairs inside a Bundle
-- is a simulation. It encodes order via binding to position names, which
is semantically correct but algebraically different. Bind-with-position
creates vectors in the `position x thought` product space. Permute
creates vectors in a ROTATED copy of the thought space. These are
isomorphic in theory but differ in their interference properties: bound
position-thought pairs produce cross-terms under cosine similarity that
rotated vectors do not. The permutation approach is cleaner because
`cos(permute(v, i), permute(w, j)) = cos(v, w)` when `i = j` and
approximately 0 when `i != j`. The bind approach does not have this
orthogonality property as cleanly.

Add it. It is a genuine seventh generator.

## Question 9: Composability

The diagram I need to check:

```
Sequential(children) ──encode──> v_seq ∈ V
                                  │
                                  │ (outer Bundle)
                                  ▼
Bundle(v_seq, v_trade, v_market) ──> v_thought ∈ V
```

Does this commute? Does the composition close?

Permutation is an isometry: `||permute(v, k)|| = ||v||` and within a
single permutation level, `cos(permute(v, k), permute(w, k)) = cos(v, w)`.
The inner product is preserved. Good.

When permuted vectors are bundled (inside Sequential), the resulting
`v_seq` is a superposition of vectors from DIFFERENT rotated subspaces.
Each `permute(child_i, i)` lives in a distinct rotated copy of the
vector space. These copies are approximately orthogonal in high
dimensions (this is the whole point of positional encoding in VSA).

When `v_seq` is then bundled with non-permuted vectors `v_trade` and
`v_market` in the outer Bundle, the cross-terms are:

- `cos(v_seq, v_trade)`: this measures how much the SUPERPOSITION of
  rotated child vectors correlates with the trade atoms. Since the
  rotated subspaces are approximately orthogonal to the unrotated
  space, this cross-term is small. The sequential content does not
  interfere with the trade content. This is exactly what you want.

- `cos(v_seq, v_market)`: same argument. Small cross-term.

The composition closes. The sequential vector occupies a region of
the vector space that is approximately orthogonal to the non-sequential
components. The Bundle operation (majority vote) preserves all
components when they don't destructively interfere. Since the permuted
components and the unpermuted components live in approximately
independent subspaces, the information is preserved.

One caveat: the `permute(child, 0)` case. When `i = 0`, most
implementations define `permute(v, 0) = v` (identity rotation). This
means child_0 of the Sequential is NOT rotated -- it lives in the
SAME subspace as the non-sequential atoms. If child_0 happens to
share atom names with the trade or market vocabulary, there will be
constructive or destructive interference at position 0. This is
either a feature (the "most recent" item in the sequence has maximum
affinity with the current state) or a bug (position 0 contaminates
the outer bundle).

**Check your permute implementation.** If `permute(v, 0) = v`, consider
whether position 0 should be special or whether the sequence should
start at `permute(v, 1)` to maintain full orthogonality. This is a
design choice, not a defect. But the diagram only commutes cleanly
if you are aware of this corner.

## Question 10: Caching

The natural factoring exists and it is elegant.

The ThoughtEncoder already caches at every AST node. The `encode`
method checks `self.compositions.get(ast)` before recursing. Cache
misses flow up as values. This architecture composes perfectly with
Sequential.

The factoring:

1. **Cache each child independently.** A pivot thought (Bundle of
   scalar atoms) is itself a cacheable AST node. Between candles,
   pivot thoughts that haven't changed will cache-hit. Only the
   newest pivot/gap thought (just entered the sequence) will
   cache-miss.

2. **Do NOT cache the Sequential as a whole.** The Sequential's
   value changes every time a new pivot arrives (the children shift
   by one position, so every child gets a new permutation index).
   Even if all children are cache-hits individually, the Sequential
   result is different because the positional permutations changed.
   Caching the whole Sequential would produce stale results.

3. **The composition: evaluate children (cached), permute (cheap),
   bundle (cheap).** The expensive part is encoding the children.
   Permute is O(n) -- a memcpy with offset. Bundle is O(n*d) where
   d is dimension and n is the child count. For 10-20 children at
   4096 dimensions, this is trivially fast.

This is a natural transformation in the categorical sense. The caching
functor `C: AST -> (Vector, Misses)` commutes with the Sequential
constructor:

```
C(Sequential(c_0, ..., c_n))
  = bundle(permute(C(c_0), 0), ..., permute(C(c_n), n))
  = bundle(permute(cached_or_compute(c_0), 0), ..., permute(cached_or_compute(c_n), n))
```

The naturality square commutes because permute and bundle are pure
functions of their inputs. Caching the children and composing lazily
is EXACTLY the right factoring. The IncrementalBundle optimization
does NOT apply here (because the positional permutations change when
the sequence shifts), but the per-child composition cache does all
the heavy lifting.

**One implementation note:** the current `encode` method pushes
`(ast.clone(), result.clone())` as a miss for every cache-miss node,
including the Sequential itself. For Sequential, this cache entry
will be invalidated on the next candle (when the sequence shifts).
This is harmless -- the entry will simply never be hit again and will
occupy cache space until eviction. But if you want to be precise,
you could skip caching the Sequential node itself and only cache its
children. The children are the stable objects. The Sequential is
ephemeral.

## Summary

The algebra gains a genuine new generator. The diagram commutes
(with a caveat at position 0). The caching factors naturally through
the existing architecture. The proposal is algebraically sound.

The one thing I would want before implementation: a clear statement
of whether `permute(v, 0) = v` (identity) or `permute(v, 0)` is a
nontrivial rotation. This determines the interference properties at
the boundary between Sequential and the outer Bundle. Both choices
are defensible. But the choice should be explicit, not accidental.
