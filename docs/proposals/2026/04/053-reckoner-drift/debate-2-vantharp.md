## What settled

Three things settled completely across two rounds.

**The discrete/continuous distinction.** Every voice arrived at
this independently and nobody challenged it in debate. Classification
needs a boundary. Regression needs full geometry. Subspace rotation
preserves boundaries approximately while destroying geometry totally.
This is why the market observer survives and the position observer
does not. Settled.

**Annotate, not transform.** Hickey introduced this in round one.
By debate, every voice had adopted it. The noise subspace produces
a scalar anomaly score. That score enters the thought as a vocabulary
atom. The subspace does not reshape the thought vector. The reckoner
sees raw thought plus one scalar fact about unusualness. The subspace
becomes a peer contributor, not a pipeline gatekeeper. Settled.

**No engram synchronization.** All five said this in review. All
five held it through debate. The engram path solves a synchronization
problem that dissolves when you remove the coupling. Engrams have a
future for regime-specific learning. Not this proposal. Settled.

## What I take from each voice

**From Seykota:** the observation that 10 buckets with ~100 effective
observations each may not be enough resolution even AFTER the drift
is fixed. He raised this in debate and nobody challenged it. The
ablation will show whether raw thoughts stabilize the error. If the
error stabilizes but remains high, bucket resolution is the next
problem. That is downstream. But I note it.

**From Wyckoff:** the correction on "one line change." He retracted
it himself. The conceptual change is small. The seams are not. The
paper trade stores the thought vector. The simulation computes
optimal distances. Both must use the same input space. Count the
seams before you estimate the effort.

**From Hickey:** the scalar anomaly score as one-dimensional
non-stationarity versus the anomaly vector as n-dimensional
non-stationarity. The reckoner can tolerate one drifting dimension
among thousands of stable ones. It cannot tolerate thousands of
drifting dimensions. This is why the annotation approach works where
the transformation approach fails. Not just simpler. Dimensionally
safer.

**From Beckman:** the 91% initial error is already the mechanism in
its infancy. There is no "good period" to preserve. The entire
history of learning under the anomaly pipeline is compromised. The
ablation should show improvement at ALL points in the run, not just
the tail. That changes the experimental expectation. If the ablation
shows improvement only late in the run, we have misdiagnosed
something.

## The R-multiple argument stands

Nobody challenged it. Nobody needed to amend it. The exit distances
define 1R. If the reckoner predicts distances that are 7x wrong,
the system does not know what 1R is. If you do not know 1R, you
cannot compute expectancy. If you cannot compute expectancy, position
sizing is random gambling wearing a suit.

This is not one concern among many. This is THE concern. Direction
prediction, noise stripping, subspace evolution -- those are
interesting substrate questions. The R-multiple integrity is the
trading question. Everything the enterprise does -- Kelly sizing,
capital allocation, risk management -- depends on knowing what 1R
actually is. Fix this first. Measure it after. Confirm the risk
estimates are honest.

---

## The final prescription

Five voices. Two rounds. One prescription, refined to clarity.

1. **Run the ablation.** Position observer with raw thought input.
   Measure trail error and stop error at 1K, 5K, 10K, 50K, 100K.
   Expect improvement at all points, not just the tail.

2. **If confirmed: feed raw thoughts to position reckoners.** The
   noise subspace stays alive. It produces a scalar anomaly score
   that enters the thought as a vocabulary atom. It does not
   transform the thought vector. Annotation, not transformation.

3. **Measure predicted vs actual stop distance after the fix.**
   The ablation confirms the cause. The R-multiple correlation
   confirms the cure. Both measurements are required.

4. **Measure the market observer's accuracy over time, partitioned
   by conviction level.** If drift affects only low-conviction
   predictions, the curve is already compensating. If it affects
   all levels, the same fix applies.

5. **Do not build engram synchronization.** Remove the coupling.
   Do not manage it.
